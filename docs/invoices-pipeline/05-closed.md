# Closed

## Overview

The invoice is fully processed and no further action is pending for that billing event. This stage covers final reconciliation, tracker updates, and capturing any lessons for future billing cycles.

## Key Tasks / Checklist

- [ ] Update the invoice tracker to closed status with final amounts and dates
- [ ] Reconcile the closed invoice against the project financial status and budget
- [ ] Note any recurring issues or patterns for future billing cycles
- [ ] Confirm payment status if applicable
- [ ] Archive the complete invoice record (vendor invoice, ONI invoice, backup, submission confirmation, closure confirmation)

## Documents Needed or Produced

| Document | Direction | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Closed Invoice Record | Output | Final record of the invoice including all associated documents and status |
| Reconciliation Notes | Output | Notes on how the invoice aligns with overall project financials and any adjustments |

## Common Hangups & Resolutions

| Hangup | Resolution |
|---|---|
| Payment status uncertainty -- unclear whether ACC-RI has completed processing | Follow up with ACC-RI billing POC to confirm payment has been issued or is in queue |
| Inconsistent tracker updates between PM and finance | Designate a single owner for tracker updates at closure, or establish a brief reconciliation check between PM and finance before marking closed |
| Closed amount differs from submitted amount due to adjustments | Document the reason for any difference and update both the tracker and project financials accordingly |

## Stakeholders & Roles

| Stakeholder | Role |
|---|---|
| ONI PM | Confirms closure, reconciles against project status, captures lessons learned |
| ONI Finance | Updates tracker, confirms payment status, archives records |
| ACC-RI | Provides confirmation of processing or payment completion |

## Typical Timeline

Closure should be recorded after confirmation that ACC-RI has processed the invoice and no further action is required. This may lag behind submission by weeks depending on ACC-RI processing timelines.

## Tips & Lessons Learned

- **Link closed invoices back to milestone completion history for a clean audit trail.** When an audit or review happens months later, the connection between what was delivered, what was accepted, and what was billed should be immediately traceable.
- Do not leave invoices in a submitted-but-not-closed state indefinitely -- set a follow-up cadence to confirm closure.
- Use the closure step as an opportunity to update cumulative project financials so the team always has an accurate picture of funds billed and remaining.
- If the same issues recur across billing cycles (missing backup, CLIN mismatches, etc.), address the root cause with the vendor or internally rather than fixing it each time.
- Keep the invoice tracker current -- it is the primary tool for answering "where are we financially" at any point in the project.
