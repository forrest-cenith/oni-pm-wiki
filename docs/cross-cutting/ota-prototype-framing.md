# OTA Prototype Framing Guide

## What is "Prototype" Under OTA?
The OTA concept of "prototype" is deliberately broad and can include:
- Proof of concept
- Model
- Process or business process improvement
- Pilot or novel application of commercial technology
- Agile development
- Creation, design, development, or demonstration of operational utility

## Drafting Rules for OTA Challenges
- Replace "Request for Information" with a marketplace challenge posture
- Use action verbs: **prototype, demonstrate, validate, integrate, test**
- Maintain broad aperture in the public challenge
- Put specificity in LOEs and later SOW alignment
- The challenge should focus on the operational **need**, not overdefine the solution
- Industry should be encouraged to submit **novel approaches** and not be constrained by traditional acquisition framing

## Four Required Challenge Fields
Every marketplace challenge must include:
1. **Description** — What the challenge is about at a high level
2. **Overall Objective** — What the government hopes to achieve
3. **Problem Statement** — The specific operational gap or mission need
4. **Desired Solution** — What kinds of approaches or capabilities are sought (kept broad)

## Challenge vs. LOE — When to Use Which
| Aspect | Challenge | LOE |
|--------|-----------|-----|
| Audience | Public-facing, broad industry | Selected vendors or narrower scope |
| Specificity | Broad — mission need and prototype focus | More specific — severable work packages |
| Purpose | Attract diverse submissions | Define contractable work units |
| Downstream use | Discovery and selection | Flows into SOW and agreement |

## Common Framing Mistakes
| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Using FAR/RFI procurement language | Rewrite around prototype need and novel approaches |
| Challenge is too narrow / pre-selects a solution | Describe the mission problem, not the design answer |
| "Prototype" language bolted on at the end | Use prototype framing from the first sentence |
| Challenge and LOEs are inconsistent | Align before posting — inconsistency creates bad proposals |
| Government requirement language inherited verbatim | Translate into OTA-appropriate challenge language |

## Key Principle
> Keep the public challenge broad, prototype-focused, and centered on the operational need — not a prescriptive solution. Government can specify more detail in LOEs/SOW later.
