# Escalation Paths

## General Escalation Ladder
Escalate in order — start at the working level and move up as needed:

### Level 1: Working Level
- PM to vendor working-level POC
- PM to government working-level POC
- **Use for:** Routine questions, document requests, clarifications

### Level 2: Business / Technical Lead
- PM to government business lead
- PM to ONI senior analyst / leadership
- **Use for:** Ambiguous feedback, missed deadlines, scope disagreements

### Level 3: Contracting / Formal
- PM to ACC-RI POC for contracting clarification
- Government to government contracting authority
- **Use for:** Contract interpretation, modification authorization, formal disputes

## Escalation by Issue Type

| Issue | First Try | Escalate To |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Vague discrepancy feedback from government | Ask for specific redlines/bullet changes | Government business lead |
| Vendor not responding to revision requests | Direct follow-up with firm deadline | ONI leadership to vendor leadership |
| ACC-RI processing delay | Status inquiry to ACC-RI POC | ONI leadership coordination |
| Scope dispute between vendor and government | Written comparison to government SOW | Government contracting authority |
| Invoice not processing | Check with ACC-RI billing POC | ONI finance escalation |
| Cancelled meetings / lost momentum | Replace with written action memo | ONI leadership nudge to government |

## Key Rules
- Always get **written direction** for scope disputes before acting
- For invoice disputes, **reconcile to contract/CLIN documentation** before escalating finance-side
- Never escalate verbally only — follow up in writing
- Document every escalation and resolution for the project record
