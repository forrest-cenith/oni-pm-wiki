# Executing

## Overview

The contract is active and work is underway. The ONI PM monitors milestones and deliverables, tracks burn rate against the schedule and billing, escalates blockers quickly, manages communication discipline between the customer and vendor, and keeps the status tracker current. This phase runs through the entire period of performance and requires consistent attention to prevent drift between execution status, contract status, and invoice status.

## Key Tasks / Checklist

- [ ] Monitor milestones and deliverables against the SOW schedule
- [ ] Track burn rate against planned schedule and billing
- [ ] Escalate blockers and risks quickly to the appropriate stakeholder
- [ ] Manage customer-vendor communication discipline (clear channels, documented decisions)
- [ ] Keep the status tracker current with deliverable status, action items, and risk flags
- [ ] Review and approve invoices with supporting delivery evidence
- [ ] Conduct recurring status meetings with vendor and government
- [ ] Document meeting notes and action items after every status call
- [ ] Flag any scope, schedule, or cost deviations early for potential modification
- [ ] Maintain a running log of delivered items and government acceptance

## Common Hangups & Resolutions

| Hangup | Resolution |
|---|---|
| Misaligned expectations between customer and vendor | Re-anchor both parties to the SOW. If expectations have drifted, address it in a status meeting and document the agreed path forward. |
| Loose action tracking (items falling through the cracks) | Maintain a single action item tracker reviewed at every status meeting. Assign owners and due dates to every item. |
| Delay in invoice approvals due to missing delivery evidence | Require the vendor to submit invoices with specific references to delivered items and SOW sections. Reject invoices that lack supporting evidence. |
| Vendor reporting is vague or inconsistent | Provide a status reporting template and require the vendor to use it. Review the first submission and give feedback. |
| Scope drift without a modification | Flag any work that appears outside the current SOW and route it through the modification process before it continues. |
| Burn rate out of sync with deliverable progress | Compare percent of funding burned against percent of deliverables completed at every status review. Escalate significant mismatches early. |

## Stakeholders & Roles

| Stakeholder | Role |
|---|---|
| Vendor | Performs work, delivers against SOW, submits invoices with supporting evidence, attends status meetings |
| Government Sponsor | Receives deliverables, provides acceptance/feedback, raises new requirements through the PM |
| ONI PM | Monitors execution, tracks status, manages communication, reviews invoices, escalates issues |
| ACC-RI | Engaged as needed for contract administration, modifications, and payment processing |

## Typical Timeline

Runs through the entire period of performance as defined in the executed agreement. Execution monitoring is continuous and does not pause. Status cadence (weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly) should be established at kickoff and maintained throughout.

## Tips & Lessons Learned

- **Link execution status, contract status, and invoice status so none drift apart.** If deliverables are behind but invoices are current, something is wrong. If the contract is being modified but the status tracker still shows old scope, confusion will follow.
- Establish a clear deliverable acceptance process at kickoff -- who reviews, what constitutes acceptance, and how acceptance is documented.
- Do not let status meetings become purely informational. Use them to drive action items and decisions.
- Keep a running risk register and review it at every status meeting. Risks that are not tracked tend to become surprises.
- If the vendor is underperforming, document it early and escalate through the proper channels. Waiting until the end of the POP to raise performance issues limits your options.
- Invoice review is a contract management function, not just a finance function. The PM should verify that invoiced items match delivered work.
