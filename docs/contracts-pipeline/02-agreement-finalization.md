# Project Agreement Being Finalized

## Overview

The contracting package is being assembled and routed for final execution after the SOW, proposal, and administrative package are acceptable. This stage focuses on ensuring all required documents are present, data is consistent across the package, and the agreement is ready for signatures. The ONI PM coordinates between the vendor, government sponsor, and ACC-RI to close out any remaining paperwork gaps.

## Key Tasks / Checklist

- [ ] Ensure all required documents are present in the package
- [ ] Confirm vendor administrative forms received (W-9, reps and certs, etc.)
- [ ] Confirm agreement data matches the final SOW and proposal (cost, scope, POP, deliverables)
- [ ] Coordinate with ACC-RI on any remaining paperwork or corrections
- [ ] Track signature and status progression through the approval chain
- [ ] Resolve any discrepancies flagged during ACC-RI review
- [ ] Confirm funding documentation is in order
- [ ] Notify stakeholders of expected execution timeline

## Documents Needed or Produced

| Document | Direction | Notes |

## Common Hangups & Resolutions

| Hangup | Resolution |
|---|---|
| Missing forms (W-9, reps and certs, etc.) | Maintain a checklist of required vendor admin forms and follow up directly with the vendor POC; do not wait for ACC-RI to flag the gap |
| Cost mismatch between proposal and agreement | Cross-check total cost, CLIN structure, and period of performance between all documents before submitting the package |
| Delayed government confirmation or signature | Escalate with a specific ask and a deadline; identify who in the approval chain is holding and engage their leadership if needed |
| Stale or outdated documents circulating | Maintain one "authoritative package" version with clear version labels; retire old drafts explicitly |
| ACC-RI requests corrections after initial review | Respond quickly with corrected documents and a summary of what changed to avoid another full review cycle |

## Stakeholders & Roles

| Stakeholder | Role |
|---|---|
| ONI PM | Assembles package, coordinates across parties, tracks status |
| ACC-RI | Reviews package for compliance, routes for signature, executes agreement |
| Vendor | Provides required admin forms, confirms proposal data |
| Government Sponsor | Confirms scope/funding, provides government-side approvals |

## Typical Timeline

Several business days after a clean, complete package exists. If corrections are needed after ACC-RI review, add additional days per correction cycle. Packages with missing forms or cost mismatches can take significantly longer. Plan for at least one round of ACC-RI feedback.

## Tips & Lessons Learned

- **Maintain one "authoritative package" version** to avoid circulating stale files. Label it clearly and retire old drafts.
- Build a pre-submission checklist of every document ACC-RI requires. Missing a single form can delay the entire package.
- Cross-check cost, scope, and POP data across all documents before submission -- mismatches found during review add weeks.
- Keep a status tracker showing where the package is in the approval/signature chain so you can answer stakeholder questions immediately.
- If the vendor is slow on admin forms, escalate to their contracts team, not just the technical POC.
