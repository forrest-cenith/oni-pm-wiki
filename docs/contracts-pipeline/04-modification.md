# Modification Being Worked

## Overview

A change to scope, funding, schedule, deliverables, or administrative terms is being developed. Modifications can range from simple administrative corrections (e.g., updating a POC name) to substantive changes that affect cost, scope, or period of performance. The ONI PM captures the requested change, determines the type and impact, coordinates government approval and vendor input as needed, and routes the modification through ACC-RI for execution.

## Key Tasks / Checklist

- [ ] Capture the requested change in writing with clear rationale
- [ ] Determine if the change is a scope-neutral administrative cleanup or a substantive modification
- [ ] Get government approval for the modification (written)
- [ ] Request revised input from the vendor if the change affects scope, cost, or deliverables
- [ ] Prepare or update the modification request documentation
- [ ] Route modification package to ACC-RI for processing
- [ ] Track modification status through ACC-RI approval and execution
- [ ] Notify all parties once the modification is executed
- [ ] Update contract status tracker and project documentation to reflect the change
- [ ] Archive modification documents with the contract file

## Documents Needed or Produced

| Document | Direction | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Modification Request | Output | Written description of the change, rationale, and impact |
| Revised SOW / LOE or Funding Data | Input/Output | Updated scope or funding documents reflecting the change |
| Change Summary / Rationale | Output | Justification for the modification tied to a specific contract artifact |
| Government Approval (email or memo) | Input | Written confirmation from the government sponsor authorizing the change |
| Vendor Revised Proposal (if applicable) | Input | Updated cost/scope input from the vendor |

## Common Hangups & Resolutions

| Hangup | Resolution |
|---|---|
| Scope creep without documentation | Require every scope change to be captured in a written modification request before work proceeds. Verbal agreements to expand scope are not sufficient. |
| Work proceeding before modification is complete | Communicate clearly that work outside the current contract scope/cost/schedule is at risk until the modification is executed. Do not authorize new work verbally. |
| Invoices submitted against unmodified scope | Reject invoices that reference deliverables or costs not covered by the current executed agreement. Route through the modification first. |
| Unclear whether a change is administrative or substantive | When in doubt, treat it as substantive. Administrative mods should be limited to changes that have zero impact on scope, cost, or schedule. |
| Government slow to approve modification | Escalate with a clear summary of risk -- what work is paused or at risk while the mod is pending. |

## Stakeholders & Roles

| Stakeholder | Role |
|---|---|
| Government Sponsor | Requests or approves the modification, provides written authorization |
| ONI PM | Captures the change, coordinates inputs, routes the modification, tracks status |
| Vendor | Provides revised scope/cost input if the modification affects their work |
| ACC-RI | Processes and executes the modification |

## Typical Timeline

Variable depending on the complexity of the change and funding implications. Simple administrative modifications may take a few business days. Substantive scope or cost changes that require vendor re-proposals and government funding approvals can take several weeks or longer.

## Tips & Lessons Learned

- **Tie every modification request to a written rationale and a specific contract artifact** (e.g., "This mod changes SOW Section 3.2 to add Deliverable X"). Untethered modification requests are harder to process and easier to dispute.
- Do not let work proceed on modified scope until the modification is executed. This is the most common source of contract risk.
- Maintain a modification log that tracks every mod by number, description, status, and execution date.
- If a modification is taking too long, assess whether interim guidance or a stop-work notice is needed.
- Keep the government sponsor informed of modification status -- they are often asked by their leadership where things stand.
