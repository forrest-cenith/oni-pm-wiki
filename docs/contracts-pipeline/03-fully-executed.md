# Fully Executed

## Overview

All required approvals and signatures are complete and the agreement is executable. This is the formal transition from pre-award activity to active contract execution. The ONI PM confirms the execution date, notifies all parties, establishes the period of performance start date, and launches the execution cadence (status meetings, deliverable tracking, etc.). All executed documents are archived as the authoritative record.

## Key Tasks / Checklist

- [ ] Confirm execution date with ACC-RI
- [ ] Notify vendor and government sponsor that the agreement is fully executed
- [ ] Confirm start date and period of performance (POP)
- [ ] Distribute executed agreement documents to all parties
- [ ] Launch execution cadence (recurring meetings, reporting schedule, deliverable milestones)
- [ ] Archive executed documents in the project repository
- [ ] Schedule kickoff meeting if not already planned
- [ ] Update contract status tracker to "Fully Executed"

## Common Hangups & Resolutions

| Hangup | Resolution |
|---|---|
| Confusion between "selected," "awarded," and "fully executed" | Use precise language in all communications. "Selected" means chosen but not yet under contract. "Fully executed" means all signatures are in place and work may begin. |
| Stakeholders starting work before authority is fully in place | Do not communicate a start date until the agreement is confirmed executed. If work begins early, it is at risk and outside the contract authority. |
| Vendor unsure of reporting or deliverable expectations | Include a clear execution cadence and deliverable calendar in the kickoff package |
| Executed documents not distributed to all parties | Maintain a distribution list and confirm receipt from each party |

## Stakeholders & Roles

| Stakeholder | Role |
|---|---|
| ONI PM | Confirms execution, notifies parties, launches execution cadence, archives documents |
| ACC-RI | Provides executed agreement, confirms execution date |
| Vendor | Acknowledges execution, begins work per POP, attends kickoff |
| Government Sponsor | Acknowledges execution, confirms expectations for deliverables and reporting |

## Typical Timeline

Immediate once all signatures are complete. Notification and kickoff scheduling should happen within one to two business days of confirmed execution. Do not allow a gap between execution and communication -- stakeholders need to know the contract is live.

## Tips & Lessons Learned

- **Use precise status language.** "Selected" is not "fully executed." Miscommunication on contract status can lead to unauthorized work or missed start dates.
- Send the fully executed notification the same day or next business day after confirmation.
- Archive the executed agreement immediately -- do not rely on email attachments as the authoritative copy.
- Include a clear POC list in the kickoff package so the vendor and government know exactly who to contact for what.
- If there is any ambiguity about the start date, get written confirmation from ACC-RI before communicating it.
