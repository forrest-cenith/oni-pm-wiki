# SOW Being Finalized

## Overview

The selected vendor's proposal/SOW is being aligned to the government's desired scope and the LOEs. This is often a post-selection refinement step where the vendor's submitted proposal is compared line-by-line against the government SOW and LOEs to ensure scope, deliverables, milestones, ROM, cost, and terminology all match before the package moves into agreement finalization.

## Key Tasks / Checklist

- [ ] Compare vendor proposal against government SOW/LOEs
- [ ] Identify mismatches in scope, deliverables, milestones, ROM, cost, and terminology
- [ ] Request concrete edits from vendor with specific line-item callouts
- [ ] Receive updated SOW/proposal from vendor
- [ ] Get government confirmation that revised SOW is acceptable
- [ ] Ensure every requested edit is written and traceable (redline or comparison doc)
- [ ] Confirm ROM and milestone alignment between SOW and proposal
- [ ] File final accepted version and supporting comparison notes

## Documents Needed or Produced

| Document | Direction | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Government SOW / LOEs | Input | Baseline scope and requirements |
| Vendor Updated Proposal | Input | Vendor's revised submission addressing discrepancies |
| Revised SOW | Output | Aligned SOW acceptable to both government and vendor |
| Comparison Notes / Redlines | Output | Traceable record of every change requested and made |

## Common Hangups & Resolutions

| Hangup | Resolution |
|---|---|
| Vague discrepancy feedback from government (e.g., "this doesn't look right") | Push for specifics -- ask the government lead to identify the exact section, sentence, or number that is incorrect and what the correct version should say |
| Vendor unaware more changes are needed after a prior update | Provide precise, numbered instructions referencing specific sections; do not assume the vendor knows what changed since the last round |
| Multiple rounds of revision with no convergence | Lock down a single authoritative redline document and require all edits to be tracked against it |
| Terminology mismatch between vendor language and government language | Create a terminology mapping and require the vendor to adopt government terms in the SOW |

## Stakeholders & Roles

| Stakeholder | Role |
|---|---|
| Vendor | Revises proposal/SOW to align with government requirements |
| ONI PM | Coordinates comparison, communicates discrepancies, tracks revision status |
| Government Technical Lead | Reviews revised SOW for acceptability, provides specific feedback |
| ACC-RI Reviewer | Reviews package for contractual compliance before agreement finalization |

## Typical Timeline

A few days to several weeks depending on the clarity of government feedback, the responsiveness of the vendor, and the number of revision cycles required. Straightforward alignment with minor terminology fixes can close in under a week. Substantive scope or cost disagreements may take multiple weeks.

## Tips & Lessons Learned

- **Do not move into agreement finalization with an unstable SOW.** If the SOW is still being debated, the agreement package will need rework later.
- Track every edit request in writing. Verbal agreements on scope changes that are not captured in a redline will resurface as disputes.
- Number your discrepancy items so the vendor can respond point-by-point.
- If the government is slow to confirm acceptability, escalate early -- do not let the SOW sit in limbo.
- Keep a version log so everyone knows which SOW draft is current.
