# OTA Intro Call

## Overview
Initial engagement with the government customer to understand the operational problem, mission gap, urgency, prototype angle, desired acquisition path, and whether the need is suitable for a marketplace challenge under OTA.

## Key Tasks / Checklist
- [ ] Identify the government organization, program office, and mission owner
- [ ] Capture the operational problem in plain language
- [ ] Determine whether the customer is describing: a broad discovery problem, a prototype requirement, a test/evaluation effort, or something already mature enough for production
- [ ] Reframe FAR/RFI language into OTA/prototype language
- [ ] Ask what success looks like: discovery of new vendors, concept papers, down-select to one vendor, multiple LOEs, test support, transition to production
- [ ] Determine whether the challenge should be broad public-facing plus separate LOEs
- [ ] Confirm timeline expectations for posting, response window, evaluation, and award
- [ ] Identify whether there are classified elements that must be excluded or handled separately
- [ ] Identify rough order of magnitude (ROM), if government is willing to share it
- [ ] Clarify evaluation ownership: who reviews, who selects, what rubric or criteria are desired
- [ ] Confirm POCs: government technical lead, government contracting/business lead, ONI PM, ACC-RI coordination point if known

## Documents Needed or Produced
| Document | Purpose | Owner |
|----------|---------|-------|
| Intake Notes / Discovery Summary | Capture the government need, constraints, and acquisition intent | ONI PM |
| Marketplace Challenge Template | Public-facing challenge statement with Description, Overall Objective, Problem Statement, Desired Solution, eligibility and timeline | ONI PM (drafts with government input) |
| LOE Template | More specific severable work packages that can later be written into the eventual agreement | ONI PM + Government |

## Email Templates

### Follow-Up from OTA Intro Call
**Subject:** Follow-Up from OTA Intro Call – [Program/Challenge Name]
**To:** Government sponsor/program office; optional ONI internal stakeholders

**Body:**
```
Thank you for taking the time to discuss [operational problem/program name].

Based on our conversation, ONI will translate the stated need into:
- A broad marketplace challenge, and
- [One or more LOEs / specific work packages] if needed.

To move forward, we need the following from your team:
- [ ] Background docs / existing RFI / SOW
- [ ] Desired timeline for posting and evaluation
- [ ] Security constraints or classification considerations
- [ ] Evaluation POCs and selection criteria preferences
- [ ] ROM guidance (if available)

We will target a first draft within [X] business days of receiving these inputs. Please let us know if you have any questions.
```

### Requested Inputs for Challenge Draft
**Subject:** Requested Inputs for Marketplace Challenge Draft – [Challenge Name]
**To:** Government technical and business POCs

**Body:**
```
We are preparing to draft the marketplace challenge for [topic/program].

To ensure the challenge accurately captures your operational need, please provide:
- Source materials (existing SOW, RFI, requirements docs)
- Whether ROM should be included in the public challenge
- Formatting expectations for industry submissions
- Whether multiple LOEs are preferred
- Any additional constraints or considerations

We look forward to your inputs so we can begin drafting.
```

## Common Hangups & Resolutions

| Hangup | Resolution |
|--------|------------|
| Government provides an RFI written in FAR language | Translate it into a challenge framed around operational need and prototype exploration, not procurement boilerplate |
| Government is too specific about the solution | Broaden the challenge and move specificity into LOEs |
| Government does not understand "prototype" under OTA | Explain that "prototype" can include business process, pilot, agile development, operational utility demonstration, etc. |
| No clear evaluator / decision authority | Resolve early by naming the technical review team and selection authority |
| Classified information contaminates the draft | Split public challenge content from classified follow-on handling |

## Stakeholders & Roles
| Stakeholder | Role |
|-------------|------|
| Government Technical Sponsor | Defines operational need |
| Government Business/Program Office Lead | Shapes scope, funds, transition path |
| ONI PM | Translates need into OTA marketplace structure |
| ONI Senior Analyst / Internal Reviewer | Quality checks challenge framing and prototype language |
| ACC-RI | Not deeply active yet, but eventual award path should be anticipated |

## Typical Timeline
- **Target:** 2-5 business days from intro call to first challenge draft (after receiving inputs)
- This is the kickoff/intake stage — move quickly so the challenge can be drafted

## Tips & Lessons Learned
- Do not inherit "this is an RFI" phrasing blindly from government source docs — the challenge itself is already the request to industry
- Anchor everything in a mission gap
- Use prototype language early; do not bolt it on later
- Broad challenge first, precise LOEs second
- Confirm POCs and evaluation ownership before drafting begins
