# OTA Intro Call

## Overview
Initial engagement with the government customer to understand the operational problem, mission gap, urgency, prototype angle, desired acquisition path, and whether the need is suitable for a marketplace challenge under OTA.

## Call Script

<details>
<summary>OTA Intro Call Script &nbsp; <span class="badge badge-oni">ONI</span> → <span class="badge badge-gov">Gov Customer</span></summary>

**1. Introductions & Context**

*"Thanks for connecting with us. We're here to help translate your operational need into a marketplace challenge that reaches innovative industry partners through our OTA vehicle. Let's start with the basics."*

- Who is the government organization, program office, and mission owner?
- Who are the key POCs on your side (technical lead, business/contracting lead)?

**2. Understand the Problem**

*"Tell us about the capability gap or operational problem you're trying to solve — in plain language, not acquisition-speak."*

- What is the mission gap or operational problem?
- How urgent is this? What's driving the timeline?
- Are there classified elements we need to handle separately?

**3. Gauge Maturity**

*"It helps us to understand where you are in the process. Are you still exploring what's possible, or do you already have a specific solution in mind?"*

- Is this a broad discovery problem, a prototype requirement, a test/evaluation effort, or already mature enough for production?
- Do you have existing docs (SOW, RFI, requirements) we can work from?

**4. Define Success**

*"What does a win look like for you at the end of this challenge?"*

- Discovery of new vendors? Concept papers? Down-select to one vendor? Multiple LOEs? Test support? Transition to production?
- How many vendors do you want to engage — one, or several across separate work areas?

**5. Set Expectations**

*"Here's how the timeline typically works on our end."*

- Challenge posting window: ~3 weeks for vendor submissions
- Evaluation: government-led, ONI facilitates
- What's your desired timeline for posting, evaluation, and award?
- Who will evaluate submissions and who has selection authority?

**6. Wrap Up & Next Steps**

*"We'll take everything from today and start drafting a challenge. We'll also send you the LOE and Challenge Template to fill in your sections, plus a Market Research Report template."*

- Confirm the follow-up email will include templates
- Set target date for first draft review

</details>

## Common Hangups & Resolutions

| Hangup | What to Say |
|--------|------------|
| Government provides an RFI written in FAR language | *"We'll take your RFI and reframe it as a marketplace challenge — focused on the operational need and prototype exploration rather than procurement language. The challenge is how industry discovers you."* |
| Government is too specific about the solution | *"Let's keep the public challenge broad so we attract the widest range of innovative approaches. We can put the specific technical requirements into the LOEs — that's where vendors get the detail they need."* |
| Government does not understand "prototype" under OTA | *"Prototype under OTA is broader than you might think — it can include a pilot program, a business process improvement, agile software development, or a demonstration of operational utility. It doesn't have to be physical hardware."* |
| No clear evaluator / decision authority | *"Before we draft, let's nail down who will review submissions and who has selection authority. We need named evaluators and a decision-maker so we're not chasing approvals after the fact."* |
| Classified information contaminates the draft | *"The public challenge needs to stay unclassified. Let's separate out any classified elements — those can be handled in a follow-on discussion or a classified annex after vendors are under agreement."* |

## Typical Timeline
- **Target:** 2-5 business days from intro call to first challenge draft (after receiving inputs)
- This is the kickoff/intake stage — move quickly so the challenge can be drafted

## Tips & Lessons Learned
- Do not inherit "this is an RFI" phrasing blindly from government source docs — the challenge itself is already the request to industry
- Anchor everything in a mission gap
- Use prototype language early; do not bolt it on later
- Broad challenge first, precise LOEs second
- Confirm POCs and evaluation ownership before drafting begins
