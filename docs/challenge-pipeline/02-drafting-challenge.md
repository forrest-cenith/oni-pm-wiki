# Drafting Challenge

## Overview
ONI converts government source material into a publishable challenge using four primary fields: Description, Overall Objective, Problem Statement, and Desired Solution. The draft should be broad enough to attract varied submissions but still specific enough to describe the operational need.

## Common Hangups & Resolutions

| Hangup | Resolution |
|--------|------------|
| Draft reads like a government RFI, not an OTA challenge | Remove RFI/FAR framing and foreground prototyping and novel approaches |
| Draft is too narrow | Describe the mission problem rather than pre-selecting the design solution |
| Draft is too vague | Strengthen the Problem Statement and Desired Solution bullets |
| Government wants exact compliance formatting | Template says ONI will pass through submissions even if formatting is not followed; government decides how strictly to enforce |
| Confusion on what counts as "prototype" | Use the broader OTA concept: business process, pilot, novel application of commercial tech, agile development, demonstration of operational utility |

## Typical Timeline
- **First draft:** Within a few business days after receipt of source docs
- **Internal review + government review cycle:** ~1-2 weeks depending on responsiveness

## Tips & Lessons Learned
- Prototype/novel focus must be explicit — this is the strongest lesson from past drafting
- The challenge should ask industry for its most novel solutions, not just mirror a government RFI
- Keep the public challenge broad and let the government sort among partial or full solutions later
- Use action verbs: prototype, demonstrate, validate, integrate, test
